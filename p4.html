Improve skipping experience while watching videos on smart TVs
(M1.1, Q1, Designing with and for digital twins)

Expertise Area: Math, Data and Computing; Technology and Realization; User and Society.

Overview: This project is aimed at improving the user experience of skipping different kinds of unwanted clips (product placement as an example) with digital twins when watching videos on smart TV. To achieve this goal, the data of real-world remote control and video playing (video time, video speed, and button state) was colected in the beginning. And then the data was ana;yzed and an initial estimated stop line (where the product replacement will stop) was generated. Finally, the Smart Clip functions was activated for the video and machine learning was used to optimize the stop line based on users' operations.
In cooperation with: Biliang Wang, Xiaoyu Wang

What's for Dinner?(individual)/Using advanced AI generate gifts recommendations(group)
(M1.1, Q2, Designing with advanced AI)

Expertise Area: Math, Data and Computing; Technology and Realization

Overview(group project): A ML-enabled adaptive system (with its UI design) that recommends gifts based on receivers' gender, interests and hobbies. The primary focus of the recommender system is to provide gift recommendations for the person receiving the gifts. In order to improve the accuracy of recommendation, different machine learning models and parameterws were explored.
In cooperation with: David Lodder, Niels Winkelman
