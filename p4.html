Improve skipping experience while watching videos on smart TVs
Course introduction: Designing with and for digital twins, the goal of this course is to enable students to explore, understand, and apply digital twins in design processes. Designing with digital twins can transform traditional research, user-centered design, and service design approaches. To develop future digital and interactive products, including the metaverse, students will need a solid understanding of data handling, AI methods, and various design tools
Expertise Area: Math, Data and Computing; Technology and Realization; User and Society.

Overview: This project is aimed at improving the user experience of skipping different kinds of unwanted clips (product placement as an example) with digital twins when watching videos on smart TV. To achieve this goal, the data of real-world remote control and video playing (video time, video speed, and button state) was colected in the beginning. And then the data was ana;yzed and an initial estimated stop line (where the product replacement will stop) was generated. Finally, the Smart Clip functions was activated for the video and machine learning was used to optimize the stop line based on users' operations.
In cooperation with: Biliang Wang, Xiaoyu Wang

Reflection: My contribution: research and team creativity session 30%; prototyping 15%; data analysis 20%; final presentation and report 30%; In this elective, I had my first experience processing data with Python during the machine learning workshops. I realized that coding can be very time-consuming for me, making it crucial to have a holistic plan to complete a project efficiently. I set a goal to quickly build my programming knowledge, as I found that understanding the basic math and computing concepts behind the code isn't too difficult for me.
During the process, a playback media player was built using the software TouchDesigner. This experience made me consider a strategy for finding the right technology to realize a design. First, I could outline the functionalities I want to achieve and ask ChatGPT for possible software, applications, or technologies that could meet those needs. With those suggestions, I could then conduct further research on case studies, tutorials, or examples of these technologies, helping me develop a clearer approach to design realization. Thanks to ChatGPT.

What's for Dinner?(individual)/Using advanced AI generate gifts recommendations(group)
Course introduction: Designing with advanced AI, this course explores the cutting-edge capabilities of machine learning and artificial intelligence for designing innovative systems and experiences. Students will gain a deep understanding of classical ML algorithms and techniques, including supervised, unsupervised learning, and more, and employ these methods to design prototypes for solving real-world problems.
Expertise Area: Math, Data and Computing; Technology and Realization

Overview(group project): Individual project: What's for Dinner? An ML case that the likelihood of a person having different cuisine is predictited using linear regression algorithm. Group project: Using advanced AI generate gifts recommendations. A ML-enabled adaptive system (with its UI design) that recommends gifts based on receivers' gender, interests and hobbies. The primary focus of the recommender system is to provide gift recommendations for the person receiving the gifts. In order to improve the accuracy of recommendation, different machine learning models and parameterws were explored.
In cooperation with: David Lodder, Niels Winkelman

Reflection: My contribution in group project: Data processing 10%; predictive model building: 10%; presentation & video: 70%. This course primarily focuses on learning various machine learning models and their application, particularly in predictive tasks. We were introduced to WEKA, a free software tool for machine learning and data analysis. While understanding the ML approach and applying it in WEKA is straightforward, working with real-world datasets provided valuable hands-on experience. I learned to redesign datasets, select appropriate ML models, experiment with different approaches, and analyze the results. This practice not only enhanced my data processing skills but also highlighted the importance of effective data handling in design projects.

“Tracy”, an in-vehicle voice assistant equipped with multiple modalities
Course introduction:Conversational design with multi-modalities, This course focuses on designing conversational interactions to improve cooperation between humans and intelligent machines. Students will learn to create effective, context-appropriate modalities that enhance efficiency and enjoyment while addressing issues of frustration and trust.
Expertise Area: Technology and Realization; User and Society

Overview: "Tracy," an in-vehicle voice assistant equipped with multiple modalities specifically tailored for truck drivers. During the design process, a user study was conducted, and the results demonstrate participants' satisfaction with the use and interaction with Tracy.
In cooperation with: Luca Andraghetti, Davide Stefani

Reflection: My contribution: final presentation and video: 20%; prototype: 35%; user test and data analyze: 10%; process presentation and documentation: 40%
I like the workshops very much because there I got very practical skills like using Google-dialogflow to build a chat-bot and calling real-time weather information from other websites in the chat-bot. I realized that collaborating with relevant stakeholders during the problem-definition stage can significantly help in defining the design space and uncovering opportunities.
