M1.2 Can Tangible Interface With Embodied Metaphor Enhance People's Perception of Microtiming in Grooves?

Expertise Area: Technology and Realization; User and Society; Creativiy and Aesthetic.

Overview: A design research that employs a tangible interface which supports the user study designed to find out whether the embodied metaphor can enhance people's perception of the microtiming in grooves. According to the results, the tangible interface applied in this study does not offer a significant advantage in improving individuals' perception of microtiming in grooves but certain aspects of young individuals' ability to perceive timing in grooves have been identified

Reflection:Through this project, I have built a tangible user interface that works with the digital interface. I have learned a possible way to transmit data from Arduino to processing, to achieve the communication between tangible and digital interfaces. And a user test was conducted, I had some practice of analyzing the data in a structured and effective manner. In this process, I gained some knowledge about creating good user experience, and got more familiar with basic methods of making physical prototypes with certain materials.
